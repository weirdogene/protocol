6. Monitoring & Prognostication

General principles

  • Neurologic prognostication should not be performed prematurely
  • Effects of sedation, neuromuscular blockade, and hypothermia must be considered
  • Multimodal assessment is recommended

Laboratory monitoring during hypothermia

  • Daily: CBC, chemistry panel, coagulation profile, ESR, CRP, BNP, amylase, lipase, urinalysis, chest AP, EKG
  • Every 8 hours: electrolytes, magnesium, phosphate, calcium, BUN, serum osmolality, glucose, arterial blood gas analysis

Prognostication

  • Serum NSE: daily for 4 days after cardiac arrest
  • Neurophysiologic studies: within 3 days after cardiac arrest
    • Electroencephalogram (EEG, BS1131)
    • Median nerve somatosensory evoked potentials (BS1431)
  • Neuroimaging: between 2 days and 5 days after cardiac arrest
    • Brain MRI (RM1018) or diffusion-weighted imaging (DWI)
    • If MRI is not available, obtain non-contrast brain CT
