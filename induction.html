3. Induction Phase

Induction

Induction should be performed as soon as possible to reach the target temperature.

  • Insert esophageal temperature sensor or foley catheter temperature sensor → connect to Arctic sun Ⓡ & record temperature every 1 hr
  • Arctic sun Ⓡ temperature management system: apply surface cooling pad → set to “automode” to achieve target temperature

Sedation and shivering control

1. Select one of the two options described below for sedation

  • Remifentanil and/or propofol
  • Midazolam & fentanyl combination
  • Stepwise protocol according to the Bedside Shivering Assessment Scale (BSAS)

2. For shivering that occurs despite proper sedation

  • Pethidine 25-50mg IV (Do not exceed 100mg/day)
  • If Mg < 3mg/dL, Magnesium 2g (XMGSF 2g + saline 100cc) IV over 10min
  • Shorter-acting neuromuscular blocking agents can be used for short intervals (single bolus or continuous infusion)

The Bedside Shivering Assessment Scale (BSAS)

Score Shivering Description Action
0 None No shivering noted on palpation of the masseter, neck, or chest wall None needed
1 Mild Shivering localized to the neck and/or thorax only Monitor BSAS score closely
2 Moderate Shivering involves gross movement of the upper extremities (in addition to neck and thorax) Escalate anti-shivering intervention to maintain BSAS score ≤ 1
3 Severe Shivering involves gross movements of the trunk and upper and lower extremities Bolus sedation and escalate anti-shivering intervention to maintain BSAS score ≤ 1

Stepwise protocol for shivering control for induced hypothermia

Indication Intervention Application Rationale
Standing Acetaminophen or Propacetamol (IV) Acetaminophen 650–1000 mg orally q 4–6 h or Propacetamol 1000 mg IV q 4–6 h Prevention of shivering
Standing Buspirone 15 mg via L-tube q 8 h Works synergistically with opioids to lower the shivering threshold
Standing Skin counter warming Forced-air convection warming blanket (Bair Hugger) at 43℃ Skin temperature signals partially inhibit shivering response
Standing Magnesium 2–4 g bolus with infusion 1 g/h; titrate to serum magnesium 3–4 mg/dL Cutaneous vasodilation; increases skin temperature; improves comfort
Step 1 (if BSAS > 1) Meperidine (pethidine) 25–75 mg IV, or 0.5–1.0 mg/kg/h continuous infusion Decreases the shivering threshold
Step 1 (if BSAS > 1) Dexmedetomidine 0.2–0.7 μg/kg/h continuous infusion Reduced shivering threshold; synergistic with meperidine
Step 1 (if BSAS > 1) Remifentanil 0.05–0.2 μg/kg/min continuous infusion Analgesia/sedation; careful use due to lowering seizure threshold
Step 2 (if BSAS > 2) Propofol 5–50 μg/kg/min continuous infusion No specific anti-shivering properties
Step 2 (if BSAS > 2) Rocuronium 0.6–1 mg/kg bolus with 3.3–12 μg/kg/min continuous infusion Neuromuscular blockade for refractory shivering

Complications of hypothermia

  • Coagulopathy: with temperatures below 35℃, clotting enzymes operate more slowly and platelets function less effectively
  • Infection: hypothermia impairs leukocyte function; infections are more likely to increase if hypothermia is maintained longer than 24 hours
  • Abnormal cardiac function: slows conduction; may provoke arrhythmias including bradycardia and QT prolongation; heart rate in the 40s is common at 33℃ and may not require intervention if blood pressure is acceptable
  • Hyperglycemia: insulin resistance has been noted during hypothermia
  • Cold diuresis: can cause hypovolemia, hypokalemia, hypomagnesemia, and hypophosphatemia
  • Metabolic acidosis (non-anion gap or lactic)
  • Changed drug metabolism: hypothermia slows metabolism and excretion of many drugs; duration of effect may be prolonged
